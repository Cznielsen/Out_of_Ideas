Rules

Prügel is a card game commonly played as a drinking game (at least at Aarhus University)

The rules are as follows:
Starting with the first player, each player takes a turn, turning over the topmost card of the deck.
If you turn over a black card, you take the card for yourself, if you turn over a red card, you can take it for yourself or give it to someone else.
The purpose of the game is to have a hand of 25 exactly, or to have 5 cards in your hand at the same time.
You can never draw past 25, if a card would put you above the limit of 25, it is discarded instead.
Likewise you cannot give a red card to a player if it would put them above 25. If no players can accept a red card, it is discarded instead.
Once you reach a sum of 25 in your hand, or 5 cards at once, you 'win', meaning that you discard your hand and get a point on your win tally, you then start a new hand.
The game goes on until there are no cards left in the deck, after which all cards are reshuffled, and the game starts over.
There are 2 special cards in the game: Queen of Diamonds (ruder Helge), and Jack of Spades (spar Mogens), these two cards grant an instant win when drawn, without discarding the player's hand.
Aces are high(14), unless an ace would put you over the sum of 25, in which case it is low(1) (blackjack rules).

Spar Mogens Prügel

The rules for Spar Mogens Prügel is the same as for regular Prügel, except ALL the cards are Spar Mogens.

How to play

Click the top card of the deck in order to turn over the card, if it is a black card, click it to take it, if it is red, click any player's name or hand to give them the card.
Cards cannot be given to players who cannot accept them, and cards are automatically discarded to the right if they cannot be taken.
Spar Mogens and Ruder Helge will automatically go to their own piles on the right once drawn, and wins are automatically tracked and displayed at the end of the game
